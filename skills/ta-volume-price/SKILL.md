# TA Volume-Price

## Overview

Provide concise volume-price rules for A-shares and practical confirmation logic.

## Core Volume-Price Patterns

- Treat volume up + price up as valid only after follow-through and clean structure.
- Treat volume down + price up as chip lock-up only after supply shrinks and structure holds.
- Treat volume up + price down as panic or absorption only after stabilization.
- Treat volume down + price down as low-reliability unless other signals confirm.

## Limit-Up/Limit-Down Caveats

- Treat limit-up volume as possible distribution; verify next-day behavior.
- Treat limit-down volume as forced selling; wait for stabilization before acting.

## OBV And Money Flow

- Use OBV trend to confirm price direction, not to trigger alone.
- Prefer active flow data when available: main force vs retail vs northbound.
- Treat PTC-like price-volume correlation as a confidence booster, not a primary signal.

## Volume Zones And Chip Behavior

- Mark high-volume zones as key cost areas.
- Treat price holding above the high-volume zone with shrinking sell pressure as strong lock-up.
- Treat breaks below the volume zone as failed lock-up and reduce risk.

## Practical Use Rules

- Use volume to confirm breakouts and invalidate false moves.
- Require shrinking volume on pullback and expansion on resume.

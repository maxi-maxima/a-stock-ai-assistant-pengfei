# TA Multi-Timeframe

## Overview

Provide a structured, top-down workflow for aligning signals across timeframes.

## Top-Down Framework

- Use higher timeframe to set direction, lower timeframe to time entries.
- Typical mapping: monthly/weekly for trend, daily for setup, 60m/15m for entry.
- Keep adjacent timeframes at 4-6x ratio to avoid redundancy.

## Resonance Filters

- Require 2-3 aligned signals; do not act on a single signal.
- If higher timeframe disagrees, pause or reduce position size.

## Stops By Timeframe

- Intraday stop: recent swing or channel edge to control trade risk.
- Daily stop: neckline, key MA, or breakout boundary.
- Weekly stop: major support or trendline to protect cycle-level risk.

## Time And Price Confluence

- Use Fibonacci retracements and extensions to define zones, not exact prices.
- Favor entries when time windows and price levels align.

## Practical Rules

- Prioritize quality over frequency; skip when signals conflict.
- Reduce size when only two layers align.
